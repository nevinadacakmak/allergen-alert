Facts!

On windy days, pollen from trees, grasses, and weeds can spread over large distances.
This can be particularly challenging for individuals who are sensitive to pollen.

Raindrops can capture airborne pollen and bring it down to the ground.
which results in decreased pollen levels after rainfall.

During the warmer months of the year, plants are more active and produce more pollen.
Conversely, the colder months usually witness a decline in active pollen production.
